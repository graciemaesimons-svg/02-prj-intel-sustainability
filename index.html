Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey — from founding milestones to modern sustainability achievements that shape a greener future.

Timeline

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename the newly formed company NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of technological innovation.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that powers many modern systems.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, bringing improved performance and multitasking.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

This year marked Intel's highest annual greenhouse gas emissions for operations; subsequent investments improved energy efficiency and emissions reductions.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) and sets ambitious goals for 2030.

2022

Net‑Zero By 2040

Intel commits to net‑zero (Scope 1 & 2) across operations by 2040 to accelerate climate action.

2023

Renewable Electricity

The company achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide, reducing operational emissions significantly.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its Sustainability Summit to align industry and suppliers on next‑generation sustainable manufacturing.
